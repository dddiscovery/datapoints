About

Welcome to Data Points, the technical blog series by the Data Driven Discovery Initiative (DDDI), designed to showcase the groundbreaking research of our postdoctoral fellows and data science community. This series is your gateway to innovative discoveries, presented through concise and engaging articles that make complex concepts accessible to a broad, yet technically skilled audience.

Each post is crafted to highlight domain expertise in creative and compelling ways—whether through a high-level walkthrough of a pivotal figure or diagram, or an interactive, explorable explanation. Inspired by the likes of Kieran's interactive visualizations, Google's research blog series, and VisXai's AI workshops, Data Points invites readers to dive into the forefront of data-driven discovery in just 5–10 minutes.

Our team, including staff data scientist Elena Liang, is here to support contributors with both technical and editorial guidance, ensuring that every post is as impactful and polished as the research it represents. Published articles will be shared on DDDI's website, newsletters, and social media platforms, amplifying the reach of your work while boosting your CV with a science communication highlight.

Whether you're looking to share your latest breakthrough or brainstorm ideas for compelling visualizations, Data Points is your platform to make your research shine. We're excited to collaborate and bring your ideas to life—reach out to join the conversation!

How to Contribute

Ready to contribute to Data Points? Here's how to get started:

1. Choose a compelling aspect of your research that can be effectively communicated through visuals and concise writing.

2. Draft a brief outline focusing on one key insight or methodology that would interest fellow researchers.

3. Work with our team to develop engaging visualizations and refine your narrative.

4. Submit your draft for collaborative editing and technical review.

Contact us at [email] to discuss your ideas or submit a proposal. We look forward to featuring your work!
